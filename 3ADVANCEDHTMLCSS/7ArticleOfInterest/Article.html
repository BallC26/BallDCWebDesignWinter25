Viewing The Vaccuum of Space

For centuries, people have been viewing space through telescopes. The first Telescope was created in 1608 when Dutch spectacle maker Hans Lippershey announced his invention to the world, creating it for the purpose to "view far things as if they were close up". His invention led to many breakthroughs in science and led to people making more powerful telescopes that could see further such as the Hubble Telescope which can see 13.4 billion light years away, and the James Webb telescope which can view 13.5 billion lightyears away.

How Space was created

Many theories exist on how the universe came to be. Some say the Big Bang created the universe when a point of infinite density and infinte temperature exploded, leaving the universe behind. Many observations support this theory such as after glows from the Big Bang when viewed from a telescope. Other theories suggest that God created the universe in 7 days, including light, day and night, humans, earth, space and everything in it. Scientists are entirely sure exactly how the universe came to be.
