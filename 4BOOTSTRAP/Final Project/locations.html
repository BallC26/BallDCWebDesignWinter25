Beautiful Climbing locations

Find The Spot Thats Just Right For You.

Yosemite

Considered the epicenter of the United States climbing by many, Yosemite is truly a climber’s paradise.

Red Rocks, Nevada

A gorgeous desert landscape, warm weather, and hundreds of bolted anchors make climbing in Red Rocks Canyon National Park both fast and fun.

Rocklands, Cape Town

Ranging from 650 to 3,200 feet in elevation, Rocklands is the perfect excuse to check South Africa off your bucket list!

Dolomites, Italy

For those who fancy technical alpine climbing, this remote part of northern Italy is almost unmatched.

Patagonia

Patagonia is known for its jagged peaks, low valleys, snow-covered mountains, and pristine, largely untouched-by-humans lakes and rivers.

Peak District, United Kingdom

It’s no coincidence many of Europe’s best climbers call towns close to the Peak District home.

Kalymnos, Greece

A whopping 33 feet above sea level, sport climbers from all over the world travel to this remote Greek island for world-class climbing.

Railay, Thailand

Another oceanic dream for many climbers, the pristine Railay beach boasts steep limestone cliffs posted right on the beach.

Zermatt, Switzerland

Mountaineering in the Swiss Alps is already a lifetime goal for many climbers. So why not climb the Matterhorn, the most famous peak in Switzerland (and, some say, the world)?

Mallorca, Spain

Mallorca is widely considered one of the premier beach destinations in all of Europe, but it’s also home to world-class climbing, too.

Squamish, Canada

People travel from all over to climb The Stawamus Chief, a giant granite dome with more than 500 established routes.

Fontainebleau, France

Two words: bouldering heaven. Tucked into the ancient forest of Fontainebleau—a scene of many famous French paintings—there are nearly 500 established bouldering routes in this European climbing hotbed.
