Description

School counselors are certified/licensed educators who improve student success for ALL students by implementing a comprehensive school counseling program. Appropriate duties include providing individual student academic planning and goal setting, school counseling classroom lessons based on student success standards, short-term counseling to students, referrals for long-term support, collaboration with families/teachers/ administrators/ community for student success, advocacy for students at individual education plan meetings and other student-focused meetings, data analysis to identify student issues, needs and challenges, acting as a systems change agent to improve equity, access and achievement and opportunities for all students

Average Salary

As of Mar 5, 2025, the average annual pay for a School Psychologist in Ann Arbor is $90,802 a year. That works out to be approximately $43.65 an hour. This is the equivalent of $1,746/week or $7,566/month.

Employers

In Michigan, school counselors can find employment primarily with public school districts like:

  • elementary schools
  • middle schools
  • high schools
  • charter schools
  • work with non-profit organizations focused on education for youth support
  • organizations providing consultant services to schools.
